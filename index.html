Line of Best Fit Explanation

Each one of these graphs show the line of best fit running through it. The line of best fit is esentially a trend line that goes through all of the data points on the scatterplot. "Best" refers to the best approximation of the given set of data points. To calculate the line of best fit seen in each scatterplot I found the mean of the x and y values. In this case this was the height(x) and then all of the y values (Rain, Site Number, Altitude, and Temperature). Once I had the means of both values I made a for loop to calculate the slope. I used the mathematical equation for slope and used the correct variables to calculate it. The final step was to calculate the y intercept. This was done by getting the mean for the y value minus the slope times the mean of the x value. This is what it looked like in my code... yInt = (avCy-(slope*avCx)).
